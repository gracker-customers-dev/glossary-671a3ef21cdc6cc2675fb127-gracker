active attack

An attack that involves an adversary actively engaging with a network or system to disrupt, alter, or gain unauthorized access to data.

Description

In the context of network security, an active attack refers to any malicious activity that attempts to modify system resources or affect their operations. Unlike passive attacks, which simply eavesdrop on communications without altering the data, active attacks seek to take control of the system, disrupt services, or manipulate data. This can involve various techniques such as spoofing, replay attacks, denial of service (DoS), and man-in-the-middle attacks. Active attacks can lead to unauthorized data modification, service outages, and significant breaches of confidentiality and integrity. Organizations must implement robust security measures, including firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and encryption, to defend against active attacks. The consequences of such attacks can be severe, including financial loss, reputational damage, and legal liabilities. Therefore, proactive monitoring and incident response strategies are essential to mitigate the risks posed by active attacks in network environments.

Examples

  • Denial of Service (DoS) attacks that overwhelm a server with traffic, causing it to crash.
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) attacks where an attacker intercepts communications between two parties and alters the data.

Additional Information

  • Active attacks require more resources and planning compared to passive attacks.
  • Detection and response to active attacks are critical components of an organization's cybersecurity strategy.
